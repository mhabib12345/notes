Archimedes: The Greatest Mathematician of Antiquity

March 14, 2026 | Estimated reading time: 1 minutes

Archimedes

Genius of Ancient Greece

Who Was Archimedes?

  • Mathematician, physicist, engineer (287-212 BC)
  • From Syracuse, Sicily
  • One of the greatest minds in history

The Eureka Moment

While taking a bath, Archimedes discovered the principle of buoyancy and ran through the streets shouting:

"Eureka! Eureka!"

Key Contributions

Mathematics

  • Value of π (pi)
  • Volume of sphere
  • Method of exhaustion

Physics

  • Buoyancy principle
  • Law of the lever
  • Center of gravity

Archimedes' Screw

An ingenious device for raising water:

  • Still used for irrigation today
  • Simple yet effective design
  • Revolutionized water management

Famous Words

"Give me a lever long enough and a fulcrum on which to place it, and I shall move the world."

- Archimedes

Legacy

Archimedes' work influenced:

📐 Galileo
⚛️ Newton
🔭 Leonardo

His methods anticipated calculus by 2000 years!
